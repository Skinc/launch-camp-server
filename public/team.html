Launch Camp coaches are experienced entrepreneurs, educators and developers.

David Gaynor

 

David attended Olin College of Engineering where he created and taught the school's first student led course - on web development and entreprenuership. After graduating he worked at Twitter for two and a half years, before leaving to found the nonprofit School Shaped the for-profit Classadoo. In 2015 he was given the opportunity to teach a two week intersession course at Design Tech High School. He decided to teach a condensed Olin College course and see how the 9th and 10th graders would do. In short, he was blown away. Every student team founded a profitable business and most evaluated the course as one of the best they had ever taken. The second iteration of the course (which had many repeat participants) went similarly well. David realized he needed to get the course out to more students, and thus, Launch Camp was born.

Catherine Uong

 

Catherine has over 10 years of experience mentoring and running volunteer programs for middle and high school students interested in entrepreneurship and project-based learning. She is passionate about creating environments for students to take ownership of their own learning, valuing academic achievement as well as the development of social-emotional skills. Currently, Catherine is the Operations Manager at Imagine K12, an education vertical within the technology startup accelerator, Y Combinator.

Tommy Hoang

 

Tommy has been an educator for the past six years, working with students of all ages and backgrounds. His student-led classroom focuses on whole child development through interdisciplinary projects. Tommy's teaching approach centers around the lean startup process which he learned through securing a paid pilot for an edtech tool he designed last summer. He currently teaches science at East Bay Innovation Academy where he often prototypes solutions to improve communication with families.
